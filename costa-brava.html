Venetian Island Miami

location: Venetian island

This Miami Beach residence, masterfully curated by Mila Design, is a masterclass in coastal minimalism. It trades traditional beach tropes for a sophisticated, "quiet luxury" aesthetic that feels both expansive and intimately detailed.

The Living Sanctuary

The heart of the home is defined by a fluid, open-floor plan where light is the primary architect. A curved cream-colored sofa anchors the space, its soft silhouette mimicking the gentle roll of the Atlantic. It sits atop a plush white rug, accompanied by a sculptural glass coffee table supported by organic wooden spheres—a subtle nod to driftwood and sea-polished stones.

A floor-to-ceiling matte black shelving unit acts as a transparent boundary, organizing the owner's collection of art and literature without obstructing the panoramic views filtered through ethereal, sheer white drapery.

The Culinary Stage

The kitchen transition is seamless and bold. Cobalt blue cabinetry provides a striking pop of depth against the monochromatic palette, grounding the airy space with the richness of deep ocean water.

• The Island: A monolith of pale, veined marble that extends into a functional work surface.

• The Dining Area: A live-edge wooden table brings a raw, tactile warmth to the concrete floors, surrounded by sleek, low-profile black chairs.

• Artistic Anchor: A massive abstract canvas in shades of lapis and cerulean commands the wall, serving as a focal point that ties the blue of the kitchen to the rest of the living area.

The Monochromatic Suite

The design language extends into a moody, high-contrast bathroom that feels like a private spa.

• The Bathtub: A deep, matte black soaking tub sits on a raised wooden plinth, offering a dramatic contrast against the raw concrete walls.

• The Vanity: A floating charcoal-gray vanity maintains the clean lines found throughout the home, illuminated by a singular, minimalist light bar that emphasizes the room's geometric purity.

Design Philosophy: "Coastal Industrial"

Mila Design has achieved a rare balance here. By pairing industrial materials like polished concrete and black steel with soft organic textures and a Mediterranean-inspired blue, the team has created a space that is undeniably modern yet remains warm and inviting—the quintessential Miami Beach retreat for the contemporary soul.
